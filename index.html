The Story: Ganesh and Raju

1

Ganesh Pre-Arrival Ping

Ganesh, a fisherman from Rameswaram, sends a WhatsApp voice note: "Anna, 120kg seer fish, arriving in 45 minutes." This goes ONLY to his trusted buyer, Coastal Exports - never to competitors.

2

Raju at the Harbour

Raju, the procurement agent, receives the ping on his dashboard. He knows exactly what to expect before the auction even starts. He sends a voice note confirming the catch details.

3

AI Processing

Bhashini transcribes Tamil to English. Groq (Llama 3) extracts structured data: species, weight, estimated arrival. A batch record is auto-created with MPEDA-compliant KDE fields.

4

Risk Prediction

Open-Meteo weather data + route simulation + temperature stream = real-time spoilage risk score. If risk crosses 30%, the exporter gets an alert to reroute or expedite.

Judge Q&A Preparation

CoastalTrace is not creating a new relationship - it is digitizing an existing one. Ganesh already has an advance/credit arrangement with Coastal Exports. The ping is just making that old relationship faster. The traceability chain starts at the batch record (which IS shared and compliance-linked). The ping is simply the earliest possible signal in that chain.

At 200 shipments/month: WhatsApp templates cost ~$50-180, Groq costs ~$5-10, Bhashini ~$10-40, Open-Meteo commercial ~$35-50. Total: under Rs.2,000/month. This is less than 1% of the spoilage savings. The free tier gets us to pilot; the paid tier is still cheap at scale.

Because it is not a marketplace feature - it is a relationship feature. Ganesh already has an advance arrangement with Coastal Exports. The ping is just digitizing that existing trust. If we made it public, competitors would free-ride on Coastal's advance investment, and Ganesh would stop using it.

For the hackathon, we use Open-Meteo free tier for non-commercial development and pilot. For commercial deployment, we would upgrade to API Standard subscription (~30-50 EUR/month) or switch to OpenWeatherMap (free tier allows commercial use, 1,000 calls/day) or IMD free data for Indian weather.

We have a six-node vision: Fisherman (built), Procurement Agent (built), Truck Driver (simulated), Processing Plant (roadmap), Transit Vehicle (simulated), Port Handler (roadmap). We are honest about what is built vs simulated vs roadmap. This transparency builds trust and shows vision without overpromising.
